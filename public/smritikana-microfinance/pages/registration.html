MFI Registration & Licensing

Your comprehensive guide to incorporating Section 8 and NBFC Microfinance Institutions in India.

Section 8 (Non-Profit) Registration

The most popular entry point for starting a microfinance business in India. Requires no RBI approval for initial incorporation.

Why choose Section 8?

Ideal for NGOs transitioning to formal credit or new startups with limited capital. Can handle up to ₹100 Crores in loan portfolio without mandatory NBFC conversion.

Requirements Checklist

  • Minimum 2 Directors (PAN & Aadhaar mandatory)
  • Registered Office Address proof
  • Minimum capital of ₹1-2 Lakhs
  • Special Objects in MOA regarding Microfinance activities

NBFC-MFI (Profit) License

For large-scale commercial operations requiring a dedicated license from the Reserve Bank of India.

RBI Mandate: ₹5 Crore NOF

As per the latest 2025 guidelines, a minimum Net Owned Fund (NOF) of ₹5 Crores is required for new NBFC-MFI applications.
